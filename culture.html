Regional characteristics and cultural notes of India

AT A GLANCE

Key regional characteristics of India

This page summarizes regional characteristics (especially climate and landscape) from the ebook, plus brief cultural notes such as religions and everyday etiquette.

Climate characteristics

Temperature

Temperatures vary from cold in the mountains to hot in deserts and lowlands.

Seasons

Influenced by monsoon winds: a rainy season and a dry season.

Winds

The southwest monsoon brings rain, while the northeast monsoon brings the dry season.

Humidity

Coastal areas and regions with frequent rainfall have high humidity.

Regional characteristics

North (Himalayas)

Cold highlands, snow, and deep valleys. This region influences rivers and weather patterns across the country.

Indo-Gangetic Plain

Wide fertile plains shaped by major rivers. Dense settlements and agriculture grow where the soil is rich.

Deccan Plateau

A broad plateau in central-southern India. Many areas are warmer and drier than the coasts, with seasonal rainfall.

Coasts & deltas

Humid shorelines and river deltas. Fishing, ports, and coastal farming depend on monsoon winds and tides.

Northeast hills

Green hills, heavy rainfall, and rich forests. Valleys and ridges create many local microclimates.

Religions in India

Hinduism

Hindu traditions are deeply woven into daily life, arts, and festivals. Practices can vary widely between regions, communities, and temples.

Islam

Muslim communities live across many states, with historic mosques and cultural heritage shaped by centuries of trade and kingdoms.

Christianity

Christian communities exist in many regions, especially in parts of the South and Northeast, with distinctive local languages and traditions.

Sikhism

Sikh culture is closely linked with community service (seva) and shared meals (langar) in gurdwaras, welcoming people from any background.

Buddhism

Buddhist heritage is connected to key historical sites and pilgrimages. In some regions, Buddhist communities keep unique cultural customs.

Jainism

Jain communities are known for strong emphasis on non-violence and ethical living. You may notice vegetarian food traditions in many Jain areas.

Other faiths

India also has many other communities (including tribal/indigenous beliefs). Local customs are often shaped by a mix of history, language, and religion.

Everyday etiquette

Greetings & respect

A polite greeting like “Namaste” is widely understood. Showing respect to elders and using a calm tone is appreciated in many settings.

Religious places

Many temples and homes ask visitors to remove shoes. Dress modestly, follow local rules (queues, separate areas), and ask before taking photos.

Eating & using hands

In many places, the right hand is used for eating or giving/receiving items. If you are unsure, simply follow what locals do at the table.

Dress codes

Style is modern in big cities, but modest clothing is safer for temples, rural areas, and formal visits. Carrying a scarf/shawl can be helpful.

Conversation

Small talk often includes where you are from and your plans. Avoid sensitive topics if the situation feels formal, and keep your questions respectful.

Body language

Avoid pointing your feet at people or sacred objects, and keep your voice moderate. In conservative areas, public displays of affection can be seen as impolite.

Markets & prices

Bargaining can be normal in street markets, so negotiate politely and fairly. For transport or services, confirm the price first to avoid confusion.

Photos & permission

Always ask before photographing people, ceremonies, or inside religious buildings. Some sites may restrict cameras or require a ticket/fee.
