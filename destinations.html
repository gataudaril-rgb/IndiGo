Mumbai

Gateway of India

An iconic waterfront arch monument in South Mumbai, built during the British Raj and now a popular gathering point by the harbour. It’s one of the most photographed spots in Mumbai and a classic starting point for exploring the Colaba area.

It’s especially lively in the late afternoon—many visitors come for the sea breeze and to capture the skyline from the harbour side.

Colaba Causeway

A lively shopping street in Colaba, South Mumbai, known for street stalls, boutiques, cafés, and bargaining. It’s a fun place to browse accessories, souvenirs, and snacks while feeling the energy of the city.

If you like street markets, take your time to compare prices and don’t hesitate to bargain politely.

CSMVS Museum

A major Mumbai museum with art, archaeology, and historical collections (formerly the Prince of Wales Museum). The galleries offer a quick way to learn about India’s heritage through objects, paintings, and exhibits in a landmark building.

It’s a great stop when you want a calmer indoor experience—especially if the weather is hot or rainy.

  • Collections

Mumbai

Tea House

A tea house in Bandra (Mumbai) themed around Indian tea culture—an easy stop to relax and enjoy chai. It’s a cozy place to try different tea blends, take photos, and recharge before continuing your city walk.

Perfect for a short break: enjoy a warm drink, sit back, and take a few aesthetic shots of the interior.

Pinch of Spice

A popular restaurant in Agra known for North Indian dishes—often visited for a hearty meal. It’s a comfortable spot to enjoy curries and breads after a day of sightseeing.

If you want to try something familiar, this is a good place to order classic curry + naan combinations.

Mumbai

Juhu Beach

One of Mumbai’s most famous beaches on the Arabian Sea, popular for sunsets and street food. The promenade gets lively in the evening, with snack stalls and locals enjoying the sea breeze.

Come before sunset for the best vibe, and keep an eye on your belongings when the crowd gets dense.

  • Sunset
  • Street food
CategoryBeach
CityMumbai
Best forEvening walk
Agra

Taj Mahal

A 17th-century white marble mausoleum built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of Mumtaz Mahal, and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Known for its symmetry, gardens, and intricate inlay details, it’s one of the most iconic landmarks in India.

Many people visit early to avoid crowds and to see the marble glow softly in the morning light.

  • Mausoleum
  • White marble
  • UNESCO
CategoryHeritage
CityAgra
Built17th century
Agra

Mehtab Bagh

A Mughal-era charbagh garden across the Yamuna River, known for scenic views of the Taj Mahal. Many visitors come here for a calmer viewpoint—especially around golden hour—before heading back into the city.

Compared to the main complex, the atmosphere here feels quieter—great if you want slow sightseeing and scenic photos.

  • Garden
  • Across the Yamuna
  • Taj view
CategoryGarden
CityAgra
Best forScenic photos
Agra

Sadar Bazaar

A busy market area in Agra for shopping local goods and souvenirs—expect crowds and bargaining. It’s a classic place to grab small gifts, browse local products, and experience the lively street atmosphere.

Walk slowly, keep your valuables safe, and enjoy the buzz—this is where you can feel the city’s everyday rhythm.
